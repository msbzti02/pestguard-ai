WHEAT PEST MANAGEMENT GUIDE
============================
Compiled from FAO, CIMMYT, and ICARDA agricultural research.

COMMON WHEAT PESTS (relevant to IP102 dataset):

1. WHEAT APHID (Sitobion avenae / Rhopalosiphum padi)
----------------------------------------------------
Identification: Small (2mm), green to reddish-brown, found on leaves and ears
Damage: Sap sucking, honeydew, virus transmission (BYDV - Barley Yellow Dwarf Virus)
Threshold: >5 aphids per ear at flowering, or >25 per tiller at stem elongation

Chemical control:
- Pirimicarb (Pirimor): Selective aphicide, safe for beneficial insects
- Dimethoate: Systemic organophosphate
- Imidacloprid: Seed treatment for early-season protection
- Lambda-cyhalothrin: Foliar spray, broad-spectrum

Biological control:
- Parasitoid wasps: Aphidius rhopalosiphi, Aphidius ervi
- Predators: Hoverfly larvae, ladybird beetles, lacewings
- Conservation: Maintain field margins with flowering plants

Cultural control:
- Early sowing reduces exposure to autumn aphid migration
- Resistant varieties where available
- Avoid late nitrogen applications

2. WHEAT STEM SAWFLY (Cephus cinctus)
--------------------------------------
Identification: Slender wasp-like insect, 8-13mm, black with yellow bands
Damage: Larvae bore inside stems, causing lodging at harvest
Yield loss: 10-30% in heavily infested fields

Chemical control: Limited effectiveness (larvae protected inside stem)
- Pyrethroid sprays at adult emergence (narrow window, 7-10 days)

Biological control:
- Bracon cephi: Parasitoid wasp that attacks larvae inside stem
- Bracon lissogaster: Another key parasitoid

Cultural control:
- Solid-stem wheat varieties (most effective management tool)
- Shallow stubble cutting to trap overwintering larvae
- Crop rotation with non-host crops (canola, pulses)

3. HESSIAN FLY (Mayetiola destructor)
--------------------------------------
Identification: Small dark midge, 2-3mm, resembles mosquito
Damage: Larvae feed at base of leaf sheath, stunting growth
Yield loss: 5-50% depending on timing of infestation

Chemical control:
- Seed treatments: Imidacloprid, thiamethoxam
- Foliar: Generally not recommended (larvae are protected at feeding site)

Cultural control:
- Plant after "fly-free date" (specific to each region)
- Use resistant varieties (most effective approach)
- Destroy volunteer wheat plants
- Deep plowing to bury puparia

GENERAL WHEAT IPM PRINCIPLES:
- Scout fields weekly from seedling through heading stage
- Monitor aphid populations with yellow sticky traps
- Preserve natural enemies by avoiding calendar-based spraying
- Use economic thresholds before making treatment decisions
- Rotate insecticide modes of action to prevent resistance

SPRAYING SAFETY FOR WHEAT:
- Do NOT spray during flowering (harmful to pollinators)
- Wind speed must be <15 km/h
- Do NOT spray if rain expected within 4 hours
- Observe pre-harvest intervals strictly
- Temperature: Best between 10-25°C for most products

DISCLAIMER: This information is for guidance only. Consult a certified agricultural expert before application.


SUGARCANE AND BEET PEST MANAGEMENT GUIDE
==========================================

1. BEET FLY (Pegomya betae / Pegomya hyoscyami)
-------------------------------------------------
Identification: Small grey fly, 6-7mm, lays eggs on leaf undersurface
Damage: Larvae mine between leaf surfaces creating blisters/blotches
Yield loss: 15-30% in sugar beet, reduced sugar content

Chemical control:
- Seed treatment: Imidacloprid, clothianidin (most common approach)
- Foliar: Dimethoate, deltamethrin at larval emergence
- Timing: Treat when >50% of plants have mines on cotyledons

Biological control:
- Opius pallipes: Parasitoid wasp
- General predators: Ground beetles (Carabidae)

Cultural control:
- Remove weed hosts (Chenopodium, Atriplex species)
- Early sowing for vigorous seedlings that tolerate damage
- Deep plowing to bury pupae

2. SUGARCANE BORER (Diatraea saccharalis)
------------------------------------------
Identification: Straw-colored moth, wingspan 20-25mm; cream larvae with brown head
Damage: Internal stem boring, reduces sucrose content, kills growing point
Yield loss: 10-20% in severely infested fields

Chemical control:
- Chlorantraniliprole: Applied as granule or drench at stem base
- Tebufenozide: Insect growth regulator, selective for caterpillars
- Limited effectiveness once larvae are inside stem

Biological control:
- Cotesia flavipes: Most successful biocontrol agent worldwide for sugarcane borer
- Trichogramma galloi: Egg parasitoid, released in the millions
- Release rates: 100,000-200,000 Trichogramma per hectare

Cultural control:
- Use pest-free planting material (clean seed cane)
- Remove and destroy infested stalks
- Harvest at ground level, destroy stubble
- Biological control is the primary management strategy in most countries

DISCLAIMER: This information is for guidance only. Consult a certified agricultural expert before application.
